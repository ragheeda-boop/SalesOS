# SalesOS Operating System

> How the company that builds SalesOS operates.
> Last updated: Version 1.0 milestone.

---

## 1. Mission

> Improve measurable commercial outcomes for enterprise organizations through explainable decision intelligence.

---

## 2. Product Thesis

Enterprise sales organizations lack a platform that:

- Preserves ownership of business facts.
- Transforms facts into explainable knowledge.
- Measures business performance systematically.
- Delivers traceable, contextual, optional recommendations.

SalesOS proves or disproves this thesis through production evidence.

---

## 3. Decision Framework (EPC)

| Priority | Question | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Did customer outcomes improve? | Continue / Stop |
| 2 | Is the feature actually used? | Keep / Simplify |
| 3 | Did it generate economic value? | Invest / Deprioritize |
| 4 | Is the platform stable? | Maintain |

---

## 4. Release Policy

Every release must answer before development starts:

1. **What customer outcome are we improving?**
2. **What evidence will prove we succeeded?**

A release is not successful until a measurable customer outcome has improved in production.

Release Gates: Technical → Product → Customer → Business → Production.

---

## 5. Metrics

| Category | Metrics |
|----------|---------|
| Customer (outcomes) | TTI ↓, Recommendation Acceptance ↑, Active Users ↑ |
| Business (value) | BII ↑, Win Rate ↑, Sales Cycle ↓, Forecast Accuracy ↑ |

| Company (composite) | Product Impact Score (PIS) — aggregated health |

---

## 6. Customer Learning Loop

```
Observe → Measure → Learn → Prioritize → Build → Release → Validate → (back to Observe)
```

Learn is a standalone phase, not part of Build.

---

## 7. Evidence Levels

| Level | Type | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| A | Production Metrics | TTI, BII, Adoption, Win Rate |
| B | Customer Behavior | Usage patterns, Session analytics |
| C | Customer Feedback | Interviews, NPS, Support tickets |
| D | Strategic Inputs | Regulatory, Market opportunity, Competition |

Expansion decisions require Level A or B evidence. Levels C and D inform but do not drive.

---

## 8. Product Lifecycle

```
Discovery → Pilot → GA → Scale → Sunset
```

Each stage requires EPC approval to proceed.

---

## 9. Annual Review

Each year, the EPC reviews:

- Are the 10 constitutional articles still valid?
- Are the 4 cognitive layers still correct?
- Are the 6 platform metrics still the right ones?
- Is the product thesis still true?

If the thesis has been disproven, the company pivots.
If the architecture has drifted, the ARB reconvenes.
Otherwise, the operating system continues as-is.
