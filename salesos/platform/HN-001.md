# Architecture Review Board — Historical Note HN-001

**Title:** The Question Shift — Phase I Turning Point

**Date:** Phase I Close

---

## The Evolution of the Question

```
Phase 1: "How do we build a platform?"
Phase 2: "How do we build a correct platform?"
Phase 3: "How do we prove our decisions are right?"
Phase I: "How do we make the next business capability cheaper than the previous one?"
```

This is not a rephrasing. It is a shift from **systems engineering** to **institutional engineering**.

---

## The Real Achievement of Phase I

Not the documentation.
Not the test count.
Not even the Kernel freeze.

But the construction of a **Learning Loop** embedded in the development process:

```
Hypothesis → Architecture → Implementation → Benchmark → Fitness Tests → Evidence → Governance → Next Capability
```

As long as this loop runs, the platform can evolve with confidence.

---

## The Principle

> **Every new capability must leave the platform simpler to extend than it was before.**

If every Release Train achieves this, then DEI is not just a metric — it is proof that the platform improves with time instead of decaying under complexity.

## The Question That Will Open Phase II

> **"Does this business capability deserve to be part of the platform?"**

If this question remains the primary decision filter, SalesOS will have a clear mechanism for making technical and business decisions together — not just building more software.
