## Key Saudi Business Regulations

### SAGIA (Saudi Arabian General Investment Authority) — Now MISA

| Regulation | Code | Relevance |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Foreign Investment Law | Royal Decree M/1 | Governs FDI in Saudi Arabia |
| MISA License | — | Required for foreign company operations |
| Negative List | — | Sectors restricted from foreign investment |
| Regional HQ Program | RHQ | Requires HQ in Saudi for government contracts |
| Minimum Capital | SAR 500K-1M | Varies by license type |

### MISA (Ministry of Investment Saudi Arabia)

- **Investment Licenses**: Commercial, Industrial, Tourism, Real Estate, etc.
- **After-Sales Service License**: For foreign companies providing services
- **Branch vs. LLC**: Foreign companies can now establish 100% LLC
- **Virtual Company License**: For digital businesses without physical office

### CITC (Communications, Space & Technology Commission)

- **ICT License**: Required for tech companies operating in KSA
- **Data Classification**: Personal, Sensitive, Public data categories
- **Cloud Computing**: Local data residency requirements
- **Cybersecurity**: Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC) compliance

### Other Key Regulators

| Regulator | Scope | Signal for AI |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| SAMA (Central Bank) | Financial institutions | License changes = market entry/exit |
| CMA (Capital Market Authority) | Listed companies | Disclosure events = opportunity signals |
| NCA (National Competitiveness Center) | Business environment | Ranking changes = market health |
| ZATCA (Tax Authority) | VAT, tax compliance | New regulations = compliance spending |
| MODON (Industrial Authority) | Industrial zones | New zones = expansion signals |

| Saudi National Day | اليوم الوطني | Sep 23 | Government offices closed |
| Founding Day | يوم التأسيس | Feb 22 | Government offices closed |

### Islamic Holidays (Lunar Calendar — Dates Shift Annually)
| Holiday | Arabic | Duration | Business Impact |
|---------|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Eid Al-Fitr | عيد الفطر | 3-5 days | All businesses closed 1-3 days |
| Eid Al-Adha | عيد الأضحى | 4-6 days | All businesses closed 2-4 days |
| Islamic New Year | رأس السنة الهجرية | 1-2 days | Government closed |
| Prophet's Birthday | المولد النبوي | 1-2 days | Government closed |
| Ramadan | رمضان | 30 days | Reduced business hours |

### Ramadan Business Notes
- **Working hours**: Typically reduced by 2 hours/day
- **Government hours**: 10 AM - 2 PM (private sector varies)
- **Mall/commercial hours**: Often extend to late night
- **B2B sales**: Reduced activity in first 2 weeks; spike in last week before Eid
- **Decision-making**: Slower during fasting; plan proposals for post-Ramadan

### Fiscal Year
- **Government fiscal year**: January 1 - December 31
- **Saudi Aramco fiscal year**: January 1 - December 31
- **Most private companies**: Follow Gregorian calendar
- **Budget cycles**: Government budget announced in December for next year

## MASM (Saudi Market Regulator) Compliance Notes

> **Note**: MASM (المؤسسة العامة للمالية) — The Saudi Authority for Industrial Cities and Technology Zones (MODON) is the primary regulator for industrial zones. For financial markets, CMA is the regulator.

### Key Compliance Areas for B2B SaaS

| Area | Requirement | SalesOS Implication |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Data Localization | Personal data must stay in KSA | All DB hosting in Saudi data centers |
| Arabic Language | Government interfaces must support Arabic | UI/UX must be bilingual |
| E-Invoicing (ZATCA) | FATOORA compliance for B2B | Invoice integration required |
| VAT | 15% standard rate | All pricing must include VAT |
| Saudization | Nitaqat compliance | HR data fields for localization |
| Cybersecurity | ECC Level 2 minimum | Security controls documentation |
| Cloud Hosting | Approved cloud providers only | AWS, Azure, GCP in KSA regions |
