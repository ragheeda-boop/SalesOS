## 1. Introduction

### What is SalesOS?

SalesOS is an Arabic-first sales intelligence platform built for the Saudi market. It combines company data, growth signals, AI-powered recommendations, and pipeline management into a single workspace so your sales team can focus on closing deals rather than hunting for information.

### Key Value Proposition

- **Arabic-first design** — Full right-to-left (RTL) support with native Arabic company names, search, and interface.
- **Saudi market data** — Integrates with Baladi, Tameem, Adaa, Najiz, and the General Real Estate Authority to keep company profiles current.
- **AI-powered insights** — Every company profile includes a "DNA" analysis with financial health, buying intent, growth signals, and recommended next actions.
- **Unified workspace** — Company intelligence, pipeline management, activity timeline, and search all live in one place.

### Who Is This Guide For?

| Role | What This Guide Covers |
|------|----------------------|
| **Sales Representative** | Daily workflow: dashboard, company profiles, pipeline, recommendations, activity logging |
| **Sales Manager** | Team oversight, pipeline analytics, AI briefings, market trends |
| **Administrator** | Tenant management, user provisioning, billing plans, system health |

## 2. Getting Started

### Login

1. Open your browser and navigate to your SalesOS URL (provided by your administrator).
2. Enter your email address and password.
3. Click **"تسجيل الدخول"** (Login).

If this is your first time logging in, you will receive your credentials from your team administrator in this format:

```
Email:     first.last@yourcompany.sa
Password:  Provided separately
```

**Important:** Change your password after your first login via Settings > Security.

### Supported Browsers

| Browser | Minimum Version | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| Chrome | 90+ | Recommended |
| Firefox | 88+ | Supported |
| Safari | 14+ | Supported |
| Edge | 90+ | Supported |

JavaScript and cookies must be enabled. Dark mode is supported via Settings.

### First Login Experience

After logging in, you land on the **Dashboard** — the main hub of SalesOS. From here you can:

- See your top priorities at a glance (Mission Center widget)
- Review pending decisions that need your action
- Read the AI-generated daily brief
- Monitor market trends and intelligence signals
- Access any part of the application through the sidebar

### Language Preferences

SalesOS supports Arabic and English. To switch languages:

1. Locate the **language toggle button** in the top header bar (shows "EN" or "AR").
2. Click it to toggle between Arabic and English.
3. The entire interface — navigation, labels, date formats — switches immediately. RTL and LTR layouts adjust automatically.

Your language preference is remembered across sessions.

## 3. Dashboard

The dashboard is your command center. It loads automatically after login and displays a grid of widgets that summarize your most important information.

### Dashboard Widgets

#### Mission Center

The Mission Center widget is the primary overview. It shows:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Companies Tracked** | Total number of companies you are actively monitoring |
| **Active Deals** | Number of opportunities currently in your pipeline |
| **Pipeline Value** | Total monetary value of all active deals (in SAR) |
| **Signals Today** | New growth signals detected in the last 24 hours |
| **Pending Decisions** | Decisions that require your input or approval |

Below the metrics, the widget lists **priority actions** — the most urgent items ranked by importance (high, medium, low). For example:

- "Review 3 new buying signals" (high priority)
- "Follow up on 2 active deals" (medium priority)

The widget also displays a **Revenue Opportunity** card showing your total pipeline value and a progress indicator.

#### Decision Queue

The Decision Queue widget lists decisions that need your attention, sorted by urgency:

- **High priority (red dot)** — Urgent decisions that require immediate action.
- **Medium priority (orange dot)** — Important but not time-critical.
- **Low priority (grey dot)** — Routine decisions.

Each entry shows the decision title, the company it relates to, the AI confidence score, and a due date if applicable. Click any entry to navigate to the relevant company profile.

#### Intelligence Feed

The Intelligence Feed displays the latest market signals across all companies you track. Signals are color-coded by category:

| Color | Category | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Blue | Tender | A company submitted a government tender |
| Purple | Regulatory | New regulation affecting a company's sector |
| Red | Competitor | A competitor won a contract |
| Financial | Financial | A company reported strong quarterly results |
| Amber | News | Industry news relevant to your accounts |

New (unseen) signals are highlighted with a yellow background. The count of new signals appears as a badge at the top of the widget. Click any signal to view details.

#### AI Brief

The AI Brief widget provides a daily summary generated by the system's AI engine. It includes:

- A **narrative summary** of the most important developments across your portfolio.
- A list of **key highlights** — bullet points covering critical events, trends, and opportunities.

The brief updates automatically each day. If no brief is available yet, the widget shows "The brief is being prepared."

#### Market Pulse

The Market Pulse widget tracks industry-wide trends and top company movements:

- **Market Trends** — Sector-level trends with direction indicators (up, stable, down) and percentage changes.
- **Top Movers** — Companies whose scores changed the most recently, with the reason for the change.

Click any trend or mover to navigate to the relevant company or sector view.

#### Recent Activity

Recent Activity shows your latest interactions and updates across all companies, including:

- New signals detected
- Decisions made
- Data updates (e.g., new company information)
- Notes added by you or your team

Each entry displays the activity type, the company name, and a timestamp.

### Customizing Your Dashboard

The dashboard grid arranges widgets automatically based on your role and available data. If a widget has no data yet (for example, no AI brief has been generated), it shows a placeholder with instructions.

Widget data refreshes automatically. If you need to refresh manually, click the refresh button that appears on individual widgets.

## 4. Company Intelligence

Company Intelligence is the heart of SalesOS. Every company in the system gets a comprehensive profile called **Company DNA**.

### Finding a Company

There are three ways to find a company:

**Method 1: Sidebar Navigation**

1. Click **"الشركات"** (Companies) in the sidebar.
2. Browse the company list, or use the search bar to filter by company name or commercial registration (CR) number.
3. Use the status dropdown to filter by: Active, Inactive, Suspended, or Expired.
4. Click a company name to open its full profile.

**Method 2: Quick Search (Command Bar)**

1. Press `Ctrl+K` (or `Cmd+K` on Mac) to open the Command Bar.
2. Start typing the company name (Arabic or English).
3. Click the result to navigate directly to the company profile.

**Method 3: From a Widget**

Click any company name in the Dashboard widgets (Decision Queue, Intelligence Feed, Recent Activity, Market Pulse) to navigate directly to that company's profile.

### Company Profile

When you open a company profile, you see the **Company Workspace** — a tabbed interface with multiple views:

| Tab | Content |
|-----|---------|
| Overview | Company DNA summary, key metrics, and AI insights |
| Branches | Office locations and branch information |
| Licenses | Commercial licenses and their status |
| Contacts | Decision makers, key personnel, and communication channels |
| Documents | Related documents and files |
| Meetings | Scheduled and completed meetings |
| Emails | Email correspondence history |
| Tasks | Tasks associated with this company |
| Settings | Company-level configuration |

### Role Presets

At the top of the Company Workspace, you can switch between role-based views:

| Preset | What It Shows |
|--------|---------------|
| **Sales** | Pipeline, buying signals, decision makers, revenue opportunity |
| **Marketing** | Market positioning, digital presence, campaign relevance |
| **Executive** | High-level health metrics, risk assessment, strategic summary |
| **Legal** | Licenses, compliance status, regulatory signals |
| **Operations** | Branch details, operational capacity, logistics |
| **Customer Success** | Relationship health, retention signals, satisfaction scores |

Select the preset that matches your role to see the most relevant information.

### Company DNA

The Company DNA widget is a visual summary of a company's profile. It shows:

**Identity Badges:**
- Industry sector
- Business model
- Company size (startup, SME, enterprise)
- Growth pattern (accelerating, stable, declining)

**Key Metrics:**
- Number of employees
- Annual revenue
- Growth rate with trend indicator (up, stable, down)

**Health Gauges (0–100%):**

| Gauge | What It Measures |
|-------|-----------------|
| Financial Health | Revenue stability, profitability, credit signals |
| Buying Intent | Likelihood the company will purchase soon |
| Relationship Strength | Your team's engagement level with this account |
| Procurement Maturity | How sophisticated their buying process is |
| Digital Presence | Their online footprint and activity |
| Expansion Potential | Likelihood of growth into new areas |
| Data Freshness | How recently the company data was verified |

**Bottom Bar:**
- Confidence score (how reliable the DNA analysis is)
- Risk level (low, medium, high)
- Number of data sources used

### Entity Resolution

SalesOS automatically detects and merges duplicate company records. When you search for a company, the system looks across multiple data sources (Baladi, Tameem, Adaa, Najiz, and others) and consolidates information into a single **Golden Record**. This means:

- You see one complete profile, not fragmented entries.
- Commercial registration numbers are matched across sources.
- Arabic and English names are linked.

### Company Signals

Each company profile includes a **Signals Feed** showing recent growth signals:

| Severity | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Critical (red)** | Major event requiring immediate attention — e.g., a large contract win |
| **High (orange)** | Significant development — e.g., 45 new engineers hired |
| **Medium (amber)** | Notable change — e.g., expansion to a new region |
| **Low (grey)** | Minor update — e.g., new website launched |

Each signal shows its source, description, and AI confidence score.

### AI Recommendations for a Company

Within each company profile, the **AI Recommendation** widget suggests the best action to take. It displays:

- The recommended action (e.g., "Schedule a demo for Smart Energy Management")
- Reasoning behind the recommendation
- Expected revenue from the action
- Estimated time to close
- Confidence score (percentage)
- Potential impact level
- Associated risks
- Alternative actions with their confidence scores

### Timeline

The company timeline shows all interactions and events for that specific company:

| Meeting created/completed | Blue calendar | Scheduled or completed meetings |

## 5. Next Best Action (NBA) Recommendations

### What Are NBA Recommendations?

NBA recommendations are AI-generated suggestions for the best action to take with each company in your portfolio. The system analyzes company signals, your interaction history, market trends, and pipeline data to produce prioritized recommendations.

### How Recommendations Are Generated

1. The system continuously monitors growth signals (hiring, contracts, expansions, partnerships, regulations).
2. It correlates signals with your pipeline stage and interaction history.
3. It calculates a confidence score for each possible action.
4. It presents the highest-confidence recommendation with a clear explanation.

### Types of Recommendations

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Schedule a demo | Suggests a product demonstration | "Schedule a demo for Smart Energy Management" |
| Make a call | Suggests calling a decision maker | "Call the CTO about cybersecurity needs" |
| Send outreach | Suggests contacting an executive | "Reach out to the new VP of Procurement" |
| Submit proposal | Suggests sending a price proposal | "Submit proposal for Digital Transformation consulting" |
| Launch campaign | Suggests a marketing campaign | "Launch targeted campaign for the energy sector" |
| Send email sequence | Suggests a nurture sequence | "Start a 3-part email series for warm leads" |

### Accepting or Rejecting Recommendations

When you see a recommendation:

1. Review the recommendation card — it shows the action, confidence score, and reasoning.
2. Click **"Accept"** to proceed — a task is automatically created for you.
3. Click **"Reject"** if the recommendation does not apply — you will be asked to provide a reason, which helps the AI improve future suggestions.

You can view your recommendation history to track acceptance rates and their impact on your performance metrics.

## 6. Pipeline Management

### Pipeline Overview

The pipeline is a Kanban-style board that visualizes your sales opportunities across stages. Access it by clicking **"الفرص"** (Opportunities) in the sidebar.

**Pipeline Stages:**

| Stage | Label | Color | Description |
|-------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Prospecting | استكشاف | Blue | Initial identification of a potential deal |
| Qualification | تأهيل | Indigo | Verifying the opportunity meets your criteria |
| Proposal | عرض | Yellow/Amber | A formal proposal or quote has been sent |
| Negotiation | تفاوض | Orange | Active negotiation on terms and pricing |
| Closed (Won) | مغلقة (فوز) | Green | Deal won successfully |
| Closed (Lost) | مغلقة (خسارة) | Red | Deal lost — reason is recorded |

The header shows the total number of open opportunities and their combined value in SAR.

### Adding a New Opportunity

1. Click the **"+ فرصة جديدة"** (+ New Opportunity) button in the pipeline header.
2. In the dialog that opens:
   - **Company** — Start typing the company name. The system searches existing records and shows matches. Select the correct company.
   - **Opportunity Name** — Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Contract renewal for cybersecurity services").
   - **Value (SAR)** — Enter the estimated deal value.
3. Click **"إنشاء"** (Create).

The new opportunity appears in the Prospecting column.

### Moving Deals Through Stages

**Drag and drop:** Grab any opportunity card and drag it to the next stage column. The system updates the stage automatically.

**Keyboard alternative:** On mobile or if drag-and-drop is not available, use the stage controls on each card.

### Closing a Deal

**To mark a deal as Won:**
1. Drag the opportunity to the "Closed (Won)" column.
2. A confirmation dialog appears. Enter the final deal amount (which may differ from the estimated value).
3. Click **"تأكيد الفوز"** (Confirm Win).

**To mark a deal as Lost:**
1. Drag the opportunity to the "Closed (Lost)" column.
2. Enter the reason for the loss (e.g., "Price too high", "Chose competitor", "No budget").
3. Click **"تأكيد الخسارة"** (Confirm Loss).

Lost reasons are tracked for analytics and help identify patterns.

## 7. Search

SalesOS provides a powerful search engine that understands both Arabic and English, with multiple search strategies.

### Accessing Search

- Click **"البحث"** (Search) in the sidebar to open the full search page.
- Or press `Ctrl+K` / `Cmd+K` to open the **Command Bar** for quick navigation.

### Search Strategies

The search page offers three strategies:

| Strategy | Label | Best For |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Full-text** (نص كامل) | Exact keyword matching | Finding a specific company name or CR number |
| **Semantic** (دلالي) | Meaning-based matching | Finding companies by concept (e.g., "energy companies in Riyadh") |
| **Hybrid** (مختلط) | Combined approach | General-purpose search — recommended default |

Select the strategy using the toggle buttons below the search bar.

### Performing a Search

1. Type your query in the search bar (e.g., a company name, CR number, or industry keyword).
2. Select a search strategy (or leave it on "Hybrid").
3. Click **"بحث"** (Search) or press Enter.
4. Results appear with:
   - Company name (Arabic and English)
   - Commercial registration number
   - City and industry
   - Status (Active, Inactive, etc.)
   - Relevance score (percentage)

5. Click any result to open the full company profile.

### Search Facets

After a search, facet information may appear at the bottom showing result distributions by field (e.g., how many results are in each city or industry). This helps you refine your understanding of the results.

### Pagination

Search results are paginated (20 per page). Use the page navigation at the bottom to move between pages.

## 9. AI Copilot (Smart Assistant)

The AI Copilot is a conversational assistant accessible from any page. It can answer questions about your data, provide analysis, and suggest actions.

### Opening the Copilot

1. Click the **robot icon** (🤖) in the top header bar.
2. A side panel opens on the left side of the screen.

### Using the Copilot

1. Type your question in the input field at the bottom of the panel.
2. Press **Enter** or click the **Send** button.
3. The Copilot responds with an answer based on your company data.

**Example questions you can ask:**

- "What are the top opportunities in my pipeline?"
- "Which companies in Riyadh have recent hiring signals?"
- "Summarize my activity with Gulf Energy this week."
- "What is the financial health score of Atheer Telecom?"

### Context-Aware Responses

When you open the Copilot from within a specific company profile, it is automatically aware of that company's data. You can ask company-specific questions without naming the company.

## 17. FAQ

**Q: Can I search for companies in Arabic?**
A: Yes. SalesOS is built Arabic-first. You can search using Arabic company names, English names, commercial registration numbers, or industry keywords. The search engine handles Arabic text natively with proper normalization.

**Q: What do the Company DNA health scores mean?**
A: Each gauge scores a dimension of the company from 0 to 100. Green (70+) means strong, amber (40-69) means moderate, and red (below 40) means weak. The scores are computed from multiple data sources and updated regularly.

**Q: How often is company data updated?**
A: Data freshness varies by source. Government registry data is updated when official records change. AI-derived scores are recalculated daily. You can check the "Data Freshness" gauge in Company DNA for the latest update date.

**Q: What happens when I accept an NBA recommendation?**
A: A task is automatically created and added to your task list. The recommendation is marked as "accepted" in your history. You can then execute the action and mark the task as complete.

**Q: Can I see my team's pipeline?**
A: Yes. Managers can view the pipeline for all team members. The pipeline page shows all opportunities in the tenant, and you can filter by team member if needed.

**Q: How do I switch between Arabic and English?**
A: Click the language toggle button in the top header bar (labeled "EN" or "AR"). The entire interface switches immediately, including navigation, labels, and layout direction.

**Q: Is SalesOS available on mobile?**
A: Yes. SalesOS is fully responsive and works in mobile browsers. A floating menu button provides navigation on small screens. No app installation is required.

**Q: Can I export data from SalesOS?**
A: Company profiles can be exported from the Company Workspace. Contact your administrator for bulk export options or API access.

**Q: What data sources does SalesOS use?**
A: SalesOS integrates with Saudi government and commercial data sources including Baladi, Tameem, Adaa, Najiz, and the General Real Estate Authority. These sources provide company registration, licensing, and regulatory data.

**Q: How do I add a new company to the system?**
A: Go to the Companies page and click **"+ إضافة شركة"** (+ Add Company). Enter the Arabic company name, commercial registration number, and optionally the English name, city, and region. Click Save.

**Q: Can I customize which widgets appear on my dashboard?**
A: The dashboard widgets are configured based on your role and available data. Widgets that have no data show placeholder messages. As you add companies and opportunities, more widgets populate automatically.

**Q: What is the Command Bar and how do I use it?**
A: The Command Bar is a quick-access tool triggered by `Ctrl+K`. It lets you search for companies, navigate to any page, and execute commands by typing a few characters. Use arrow keys to navigate results and Enter to select.

**Q: How do I report a bug or request a feature?**
A: Use the in-app support form (click the help icon on the dashboard) to submit a bug report or feature request. Include as much detail as possible — steps to reproduce, expected behavior, and screenshots.

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes. SalesOS encrypts all data in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Authentication uses JWT tokens with role-based access control. API keys are stored securely and never committed to source code. Your data stays within your tenant and is never shared with other tenants.

**Q: Can I undo closing a deal as Won or Lost?**
A: Contact your administrator if you need to reopen a closed deal. Closed deals are archived for reporting accuracy, but administrators can restore them when necessary.
